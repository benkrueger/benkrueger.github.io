About me:

Hello, my name is Benjamin Krueger. I'm a Sr. Software Engineering Analyst at Accenture Federal Services, where I architect and develop full-stack applications, design cloud infrastructure, and build CI/CD automation for government clients.

I graduated from University Of Missouri St Louis in August 2022 with a B.S. in Computer Science (GPA: 3.25), after transferring from Missouri University of Science and Technology. My professional journey has taken me through diverse roles in software engineering and DevOps across government contracting, tech consulting, and enterprise environments.

Throughout my career, I've worked on everything from architecting Scala-based reference applications and managing enterprise GitHub instances to deploying production AWS infrastructure and building security automation pipelines. I've had the opportunity to work with organizations like Accenture Federal Services, Cisco (via TEKSystems), Omni Federal, the United States Geological Survey, and Hunter Engineering, gaining experience across the full software development lifecycle.

My current focus is on building maintainable, scalable systems that balance technical excellence with developer productivity. I particularly enjoy working at the intersection of software architecture, DevOps practices, and security automation - areas where thoughtful design can have significant impact.

This site serves as my personal webpage, portfolio, and knowledge base. While it may not always be up-to-date, I use it to showcase projects, document technical interests, and share insights from my work.
